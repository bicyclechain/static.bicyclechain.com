Bob Moccia

My first bike was a garage sale special my parents bought for me some years ago. There wasnâ€™t anything particularly special about it, but to a five year old who pretended he was on a motorcycle as he ran on the trails behind the school at recess time, this black and yellow prize was the key that unlocked my passion for the pedal powered two wheeled machine. A few years later I was compelled to take my bike apart, you know, to see how it worked. With the help of a few basic tools in the garage, I found myself looking at the parts that comprise a bicycle, all neatly arranged on the driveway. Needless to say Dad was not happy with his new task to reassemble my bike and from that point forward I was able to do it on my own. My relationship with this shop started on October 14, 2000 when I bought my first bike from the Bicycle Chain. I started riding with purpose, hired a cycling coach and started participating in club rides. I joined a cycling team and somewhere during this time I realized what I had known all along, that I love bikes and the people that surround the cycling community. I applied at the Bicycle Chain and here I am today. My purpose at the Chain is to guide people in their quest to enhance their experience on the bike whether it is riding around Como Lake on a Sunday afternoon or joining a casual Saturday morning shop ride. I like being a part of this community and I look forward to contributing to the health and well-being of our customers in the years to come.
