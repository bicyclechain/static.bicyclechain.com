I shouldn't be alive...

Submitted by Bryce Rasmussen on Sat, 08/25/2012 - 06:42

It all started with a phone call...

“RING RING”

Me: Oh crap! What did I screw up this time?

Tim: Well...nothing that I can think of...I’m calling on a more personal note...

Tim had called me a few times this past week, mostly regarding loose ends that I had failed to tie off - and I had assumed he was inquiring on some half-assed note I had left him...

Me: Well that’s a relief - I’m surprised! What’s up?

Tim: I was just wondering if you’d like to go for a run along the river bottoms on Thursday.

Me: I suppose, I don’t have anything else planned...

Tim: Cool! What percent are you?

I’m a flakey kind of person. I agree to most things if I like the idea of it - not necessarily if I can attend. Tim has endured the full brunt of this, the pinnacle of which I backed out of my own birthday gravel ride somewhat last-minute.

Me: 180%

Tim: Awesome. Are you OK in your Five-Fingers?

Me: Why wouldn’t I be?

Tim: Ok - just wondering - see you then!

**End**

And THAT is how it all started - the reason why I Shouldn’t be alive.

We started at 8. A meet at Lake street and “the river.” We biked a meager 12 miles to the trail entry south of the airport. Upon entry to the trail it was a little unbelievable how far we bike in until the run was supposed to begin - around 4.5 miles to be precise (thanks Garmin).

Tim had opted for the A-Train - a good choice, but was running particularly skinny tires for the loose sand. Fat bikes would have been the safe choice - although slow. He had to walk a fair amount of the sand, while I plowed through on my Space-Cow.

Once arrived at the start, we locked our bikes, changed our shoes, and began the ‘real’ adventure. We started running. Being the inexperienced runner I am, I was initially surprised at the slow pace we were going.

Tim: We’re running kind of fast...

Me: Really?! I can’t tell...

We ran like that for several miles - the first being just over nine minutes - progressing to 10:30. and then....

Me: Tim, I know you’re going to think I’m being stupid, but I really don’t think I can make it back once we get to BK.

We had already gone almost 5 miles - 2 miles further than I had ever run in my life - and I was feeling it in a bad way. My legs were just not there - it started feeling like my legs were separate from my body - like they were my bike, but just moving slowly - not the way I wanted. My feet were starting to feel raw - the blisters starting on the arch, heel, and bottom. My hips were not able to lift my legs anymore, and my left knee in particular was feeling the movement in a bad way.

Tim: Well, what do you want to do? We can turn around now, but it’s going to suck.

Me: How far is BK?

Tim: 3 miles...

Me: Lets just keep going...

One more mile is all I made it, my mile time slowed to a meager 11:30 and that was all I had - the matches were gone.

Me: Can we walk for a while?

Tim: Sure.

So we walked. I had not realized how badly I actually felt until I stopped running. Ow. This hurt!

I soon realized that 3 miles was further than I wanted to walk, so we decided on a closer food venue - Chipolte looked about 2 miles away. We walked through the suburban paradise that is Chakopee, through a Masonic community when Doug called.

Me: Hey Doug!

Doug: Whassup?!!

Me: You know that run Tim invited me on? I blew up!

Doug: Oh no!! Where are you guys?

Me: In Shakopee - we’re headed to Chipotle.

Doug: Need me to pick you up?

Now Doug is the nicest guy you’ll ever meet. He is a bit older than Tim and myself, but we consider him a close friend. He can hold his own and is a fantastic inspiration to me as I get older. I want to be as fit as Doug at half his age. Anyway - super nice.

Me: I love you, Doug.

Doug: See you in a bit.

So we get to Chipotle, buy burritos for ourselves and Doug, and doug meets us and we have a lovely lunch together. Doug then gives us a ride to the bridge where we left our bikes.

Us: Thanks Doug!

We depart. We find the closest path, and start walking down towards the river.

Tim: Oh sh*t!

Me: What?!

It had been about 30 minutes. We had walked for a while, carefuly choosing paths that we thought were the most direct to where our bikes were parked.

Tim: We’re on the wrong side of the lake.

Me: What?

Tim: There’s a lake too, in addition to the river.

Me: Crap. So what are our options?

Tim: Well, we can walk around the lake, cross the freeway bridge with no real pedestrian crossing, or try to cross the closed/condemned railroad bridge.


Me: ...I don’t know.

Tim: Well, I think walking around is the safe bet.

Me: Yeah, with the way our luck has been today we shouldn’t take any risks...

So we walked, and walked, and walked and walked. We stopped half way to re-fuel ourselves at a 'Crown Royal' and get more water and then continued. Tim kept sharing stories about how this reminded him of 'I shouldn't be alive.' We kept on in the heat until we saw our bikes.

Me: I can’t believe it, Tim, but I think our adventure is coming to an end.

We grabbed our bikes, biked the 3.5 miles to the road, and took the light rail home. I napped for a few hours while Tim figured out exactly how far we walked around that damned lake (7 miles I’m told). It was an epic adventure, and I can honestly say that I shouldn't be alive!!!!!
